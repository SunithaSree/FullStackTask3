Snowwhite

Snow White is the first Disney Princess. Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs was the first full-length animated feature film ever made and one of Walt Disney’s most successful films. Her character is based on the German fairy tale, Schneewittchen. The voice actor Adriana Caselotti also provides the singing voice for Snow White. Snow White can communicate with wildlife and becomes close friends with the Seven Dwarfs. One of her favorite dwarfs is Dopey. Snow White loves the Prince, and only true love’s kiss will be able to wake her from a timeless sleep.

Cindrella

Cinderella is the second Disney princess, and her film is based on a French fairy tale called Cinderella by Charles Perrault. Two mice, Jaq and Gus, become good friends with Cinderella and help her get to the Royal Ball. The film was so successful that Disney made two animated sequels and a live-action remake of the original. Ilene Woods plays Cinderella’s speaking voice and singing voice in the original. In the sequels, the voice actress changed to Jennifer Hale, and Tami Tappin sings as Cinderella in the animated movie Cinderella III: A Twist In Time. Popular villains in Cinderella are her new stepfamily Lady Tremaine, Anastasia Tremaine, and Drizella Tremaine.

Ariel

Ariel is the only underwater princess, and the film is based on the Danish fairy tale by Hans Christian Anderson. She is also the only princess not to be born human, although sometime later, she and Eric are the first royal cartoon couple to have a child. Ariel has three close friends, Flounder, a yellow and bluefish, Sebastian, a lobster, and Scuttle, a seagull, joining her in her adventures and helping look out for her. Jodi Benson is Ariel’s speaking voice and singing voice in all three films.

Belle

Belle occasionally speaks in French. Belle was 1 of 4 princesses that were all animated by the same artist, Mark Henn. Belle is 1 of 7 caucasian princesses. There are only five minutes in Beauty and the Beast without music. Belle is the only person in her town that wears blue.

Mulan

Mulan is the second princess not based on a fairy tale. Mulan is based on Hua Mulan’s legend, who replaced her father in military service to the Emperor. Mulan’s close friends, a dragon named Mushu, and Cri-Cee, the cricket travel with Mulan throughout her adventures to guide and protect her.

Tiana

Tiana is best known for becoming the first African American Disney Princess. She resides in New Orleans and is also the first American. Her best friend Charlotte La Bouff (Lottie) unknowingly helps Tiana discover true love. Anika Noni Rose performs Tiana’s speaking voice and singing voices.

Moana

Moana is the island princess of the Motunui tribe. She is the Chief’s daughter and must overcome considerable challenges to save her people. Moana braves the ocean waves with her friends Pua the pig and Heihei the rooster. Auli’i Cravalho performs Moana’s speaking and singing voice. She won an Annie award for outstanding achievement in voice acting.

Aurora

Princess Aurora is the star of Sleeping Beauty. The film is based on a French and Italian fairy tale that is much darker than the Disney version. Mary Costa is Aurora’s speaking and singing voice with a British accent. Aurora is the only child of King Stefan and likely marries Prince Phillip.

Rapunzel

Rapunzel is an only child that lives in a tall tower based on the story by the Brothers Grimm. Although she doesn’t know many people outside her tiny world, she is best friends with a chameleon, Pascal. She is known for her long magical golden hair, which Mother Gothel uses to keep her youthful appearance. Mandy Moore performs both her speaking voice and singing voice.
